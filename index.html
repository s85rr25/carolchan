About

I am currently an Honorary Professor at the Faculty of Education, University of Hong Kong, where I have served as Co-Convenor of the Sciences of Learning Strategic Theme, advancing interdisciplinary research in learning across university faculties. I have also served as Research Leader of the Human Communication, Development, and Information Sciences Department/Unit at the Faculty, promoting research excellence and mentoring junior scholars for career development. My research in learning sciences and CSCL focuses on the knowledge building as knowledge creation model examining how learners work collectively to advance the frontiers of knowledge within their community, supported by collaborative technology.

My research in knowledge building began with my award-winning Ph.D. study, investigating how students collaborate to learn science. I worked with Professors Scardamalia and Bereiter on the development of Knowledge Forum, a pioneering CSCL environment. Over the past three decades, with my team, we have advanced the knowledge building model, stimulating and spanning three inter-related areas that contribute to learning sciences and CSCL research.

Three Inter-Related Research Areas

Assessing and Designing Knowledge Building using Reflective Assessment and Analytics

We conceptualized and designed reflective assessment for assessing and scaffolding student inquiry, with students taking agency in assessing their own collaboration and knowledge building. We have extended this approach using analytics, enriching current CSCL development in collaborative analytics.

Developing Epistemologies and Socio-Cognitive-Emotional Dynamics of Knowledge Building

We have examined epistemic-socio-cognitive processes, theorizing meta-discourse in collaboration and expanding to emotion-affective processes, enriching theories of knowledge building and collaboration.

Contextual-Cultural-Equity Considerations of Knowledge Building

We examined knowledge building with low-achieving marginal students, addressing equity issues in CSCL/learning sciences, and explore multi-level collaboration, expanding to research-practice partnerships, including working with large teacher networks and knowledge building communities globally and in Asian educational contexts.

I have published widely in high-impact journals indexed by SSCI, with my top 10 publications all ranking above the 80th percentile in Education/Educational Research. My Google Scholar h-index is now 40, indicating high citation impact, and my research has received several best paper awards. I am recognized as an expert in the field regularly invited to publish on knowledge building to synthesize key insights and directions. I have given keynotes on Knowledge building and the learning sciences at the first ISLS annual meeting and various conferences providing perspectives on past, present and future development of the field. I have obtained large interdisciplinary grants on the Science of Learning, providing research leadership for advancing research on learning across faculties, and collaborated on international grants with teams and networks. I am an associate editor of the International Journal of Computer-Supported Collaborative Learning (ijCSCL) and an editorial board member of the Journal of Learning Sciences (JLS) and various other prestigious journals on learning, cognition, and technology. Currently I also serve on the selection panel for JLS outstanding paper award.

I am an active member of the International Society of Learning Sciences (ISLS) and currently serve as Co-Chair of the CSCL Committee. Working together with ijCSCL editors and dedicated members, we seek to build a stronger identity for CSCL, reaching out to and expanding the community. Mentoring is a key theme for me, and I regularly serve as a co-chair, organizer, and mentor for early career and mid-career workshops, as well as the doctoral consortium, to support and nurture young scholars in learning sciences/CSCL. I have also received the Faculty Outstanding Graduate Student Supervisor Award. I am dedicated to integrating teaching and research, and my work on using learning sciences/CSCL in the classroom has been recognized with the University teaching fellow award.

Three Interconnected Research Areas

Reflective Assessment & Analytics

Designing tools for scaffolding student inquiry and collaborative knowledge building through data-driven insights and reflective feedback mechanisms.

Epistemologies & Socio-Cognitive-Emotional Dynamics

Theorizing meta-discourse, epistemic understanding, and affective processes in collaborative learning environments, examining how emotions shape knowledge construction.

Contextual, Cultural & Equity Considerations

Knowledge building with diverse learners, research-practice partnerships, cross-cultural educational contexts, and supporting academically low-achieving students.
